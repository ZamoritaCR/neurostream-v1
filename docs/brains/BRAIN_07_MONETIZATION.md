## 2. MONETIZATION MODELS

### Model 1: Freemium

**How It Works:**
- Free download and basic functionality
- Premium features behind paywall
- Goal: convert free users to paid

**Free Features (typical):**
- Mood tracking
- Daily quotes/tips
- Basic exercises
- Limited content library

**Premium Features (typical):**
- Full content access
- Advanced analytics
- Personalized recommendations
- Therapist/coach access
- In-depth courses

**Conversion Strategy:**
- Time-limited trials (7-14 days)
- Feature "teasers" 
- Usage-based prompts ("You've completed 5 sessions—unlock more!")
- Email/push nurture campaigns

**Success Factors:**
- Free tier must provide real value
- Clear differentiation between free/paid
- Seamless upgrade experience
- Value demonstration before paywall

### Model 2: Subscription

**Pricing Benchmarks (Mental Health Apps):**

| App | Monthly | Annual | Notes |
|-----|---------|--------|-------|
| Headspace | $12.99 | $69.99 | Meditation/sleep |
| Calm | ~$14.99 | $69.99 | Meditation/sleep |
| MindDoc | ~$10/mo | $69.99 | Mood tracking |
| BetterHelp | $240-600 | - | Live therapy |
| Wysa | $99.99/mo | - | Premium AI + coaching |
| MyFitnessPal | $9.99 | $49.99 | Health tracking |
| Noom | Varies | $149+ | Weight loss programs |

**Typical Mental Health Subscription:**
- $50-70 annually for content-based apps
- $10-15/month standard range
- Premium/coaching tiers significantly higher

**Subscription Strategy:**
- Offer monthly and annual options
- Annual discount (typically 40-50% savings)
- Free trial period before commitment
- Multiple tiers for different needs

### Model 3: In-App Purchases

**Types:**
- One-time content unlocks (courses, programs)
- Consumables (session packs, credits)
- Cosmetic upgrades (themes, avatars)
- Feature unlocks

**Pricing Examples:**
- CBT courses: $9-29
- Sleep story collections: $9-15
- Premium meditation series: $9-29
- Special features: $2.99-9.99

**Best For:**
- Users who resist subscription commitment
- Testing premium value before subscribing
- Supplementing subscription revenue

### Model 4: B2B / Enterprise

**Corporate Wellness:**
- Employers pay $200-500 per employee annually
- Employee benefits packages
- HR/wellness program integration
- Bulk licensing

**Healthcare Partnerships:**
- Insurance partnerships: $150-300 per user/year reimbursement
- Clinic/provider integrations
- Prescription digital therapeutics model

**B2B Advantages:**
- 3-5× higher ARPU than consumer
- Stable, predictable revenue
- Lower churn than individual users
- Larger contract values

### Model 5: Hybrid Models

**Combination Approaches:**
- Freemium + subscription tiers
- Subscription + in-app purchases
- Consumer + B2B channels
- App + coaching marketplace

**Successful Example (Talkspace):**
- Individual subscriptions
- Corporate partnerships (primary revenue driver)
- Insurance coverage options
- Profession-specific programs

## 3. PRICING STRATEGY FOR ADHD APPS

### Consumer Pricing Considerations

**ADHD-Specific Factors:**
- Decision fatigue with complex pricing
- Impulse purchasing patterns
- Value perception varies with symptom severity
- Price sensitivity during financial dysregulation

**Recommended Approach:**
- Simple, clear pricing tiers
- Transparent value proposition
- Low barrier to entry (free tier or low-cost trial)
- Annual option for committed users

### Pricing Psychology

**Anchoring:**
- Show monthly price first, then annual savings
- Display "most popular" option
- Compare to therapy costs ($100-200/session)

**Value Framing:**
- "Less than a cup of coffee per day"
- "Fraction of traditional therapy cost"
- "Investment in your mental health"

**Reducing Friction:**
- Free trial without credit card (if possible)
- Money-back guarantee
- Easy cancellation messaging

### Recommended Tier Structure

**Tier 1: Free**
- Basic emotion tracking
- Limited technique library (3-5 techniques)
- Daily tip/encouragement
- Purpose: Acquisition, value demonstration

**Tier 2: Premium ($6.99-9.99/month or $49.99-69.99/year)**
- Full technique library
- Personalized recommendations
- Progress analytics
- All core features
- Purpose: Main revenue driver

**Tier 3: Premium+ (Optional, $12.99-19.99/month)**
- Everything in Premium
- Coach/peer support access
- Advanced features
- Priority support
- Purpose: Higher ARPU from engaged users

## 4. CONVERSION & RETENTION METRICS

### Industry Benchmarks

**Conversion Rates:**
- Free to paid: 2-5% typical for apps
- Trial to paid: 20-40% for mental health apps with engaged users
- Mental health apps with gamification: up to 48% higher engagement

**Retention Benchmarks:**
- Day 1: 25-30% typical
- Day 7: 10-15%
- Day 30: 5-10%
- Gamified apps: 22% average improvement in retention

**ADHD App Specific:**
- Inflow: median 3.86 uses/week among engaged users
- Chatbot CBT: average 20.32 uses in 4 weeks
- High dropout is significant challenge (acknowledged in research)

### Key Metrics to Track

**Acquisition:**
- Cost per install (CPI)
- Cost per acquisition (CPA)
- Channel effectiveness
- App store conversion rate

**Activation:**
- Onboarding completion rate
- First technique attempted
- Time to first value moment

**Engagement:**
- Daily/Weekly/Monthly Active Users
- Session frequency and length
- Feature usage rates
- Technique completion rates

**Revenue:**
- Trial to paid conversion
- Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)
- Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)
- Lifetime Value (LTV)
- LTV:CAC ratio (target 3:1+)

**Retention:**
- Day 1, 7, 30, 90 retention
- Churn rate
- Cohort analysis
- Reactivation rate

## 7. REGULATORY & COMPLIANCE CONSIDERATIONS

### Data Privacy

**GDPR (European Union):**
- Strict personal data protections
- Consent requirements
- Data portability rights
- Applies if serving EU users

**HIPAA (United States):**
- Applies if handling protected health information
- May not apply to consumer wellness apps
- Required for healthcare provider integrations

**App Store Requirements:**
- Health app data handling policies
- Mental health app guidelines
- Accurate claims requirements

### Clinical Claims

**FDA Digital Therapeutics:**
- Some apps seeking FDA clearance
- Allows clinical efficacy claims
- Higher evidence bar required
- Example: EndeavorRx (FDA-cleared for ADHD)

**Without FDA Clearance:**
- Cannot claim to "treat" or "cure"
- "Support," "manage," "track" language
- Wellness vs. medical positioning
- Disclaimers required

## 9. AVOIDING MONETIZATION PITFALLS

### ADHD-Specific Concerns

**Decision Paralysis:**
- Too many pricing options overwhelms
- Keep choices simple (2-3 tiers max)
- Clear "best value" indication

**Subscription Fatigue:**
- ADHD users may forget to cancel trials
- Be ethical about auto-renewal
- Easy cancellation process
- Reminder before charge

**Impulsive Purchases → Regret:**
- Don't exploit impulsivity
- Clear value communication
- Generous refund policy
- Build trust over pressure

### Ethical Monetization

**Do:**
- Provide genuine value at every tier
- Be transparent about what's free vs. paid
- Respect user financial situations
- Consider accessibility pricing

**Don't:**
- Hide important safety resources behind paywall
- Use dark patterns to prevent cancellation
- Make crisis features premium-only
- Exploit emotional vulnerability for sales
